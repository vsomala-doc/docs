Managing Data Entry

The Data Entry Portal in EDC simplifies participant data management, allowing you to manage participant information, and track visits. The Participants page is the central access point for data entry activities, where you can view participant lists and access individual participant records for more detailed actions.

Managing participants information​

The Participants page provides a comprehensive list of all participants, displaying key information such as participant ID, date of registration, date of enrollment, status, and data entry options. This page serves as the primary interface for viewing participants’ study statuses and initiating data entry tasks.

Select All Participants under ‘Data Entry’ from the left menu of the EDC to access this page.

Expanding participant rows​

Expanding a participant row provides a detailed view of individual participant information. This feature allows Clinical Research Coordinators (CRCs) to quickly access a participant’s personal information and study resources without navigating away from the main Participants page.

Steps to view a participant's detailed information​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, select All Participants under ‘Data Entry’.

The Participants page appears, listing all participants with their corresponding details.

  1. Click the arrow icon to expand the row and view the ‘Personal Information’ and ‘Resources’ sections for that participant.

Personal Information section​

This section contains essential details for each participant, including preferred language and cohort assignments.

Resources section​

This section includes tools and options to manage the participant’s enrollment history, view visit schedules, and access treatment information.

Options available:

  • Enrollment history: Provides a record of all enrollment actions, including initial registration, cohort assignments, and advancements.
  • Visit schedule: Displays the scheduled visits for the participant, covering both protocol-specified visits and additional visits as required.

Accessing participant visit view information​

The Participant Visit View page shows the study visits assigned to a specific participant. From this page, you can view the status of existing visits and associated Case Report Forms (CRFs), or manage non-visit-based forms, as needed.

Steps to access the Participant Visit View page​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, go to ‘Data Entry’ and select Participant Visit View.
  2. Use the search bar at the top of the page to locate the participant by ID.

Selecting participants​

You can select participants to access their visit information and manage related tasks.

Steps to select participants​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, select Participant Visit View under ‘Data Entry’. The Participant Visit View page appears.
  2. Use the search bar at the top of the page to locate the participant by ID. The selected participant's visit details load.
  • The ‘Participant ID’ appears at the top-left of the page. If you have ‘View PHI’ permission, additional information such as the participant's name and date of birth will display (if collected).
  • All expected and supplementary visits for the participant are shown as tabs on the top-right of the page. Each visit tab indicates its status: Due, Overdue, Complete, or Missed.
  • The list of expected CRFs for the visit is displayed in the left pane under the Participant ID. Their status is also shown.
  1. To manage non-visit-based forms, select NON VISIT-BASED FORMS.

Visit and CRF status descriptions

The following table details the visit status, including its description and purpose.

Visit StatusDescription
DueThe visit is scheduled and is approaching its due date.
OverdueThe visit has passed its due date and is still incomplete.
CompletedThe visit has been successfully completed.
MissedThe visit was scheduled but not attended.

The following table details the CRF status, including its description and purpose.

CRF StatusDescription
To DoCRFs generated based on visit group assignments during registration, enrollment, or randomization.
In ProgressCRFs where data entry has started but the form has not yet been submitted.
CleaningCRFs that have been submitted but have open queries, either manual or automatic.
To ReviewCRFs submitted without open queries but have pending tasks such as review or verification.
DoneCRFs submitted without any open queries or pending tasks.
Expectation RemovedThe CRF had a form exception requested and approved, resulting in the CRF no longer being expected for the participant.

Managing study visits​

The Participant Visit View page allows you to access and review all scheduled and supplemental visits for a participant. From this page, you can view visit details, monitor statuses, and print schedules for your records.

Steps to view study visits​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, select Participant Visit View under ‘Data Entry’. The Participant Visit View page appears.
  2. Use the search bar to locate a participant by ID. The selected participant's details load.
    • The Participant ID is displayed at the top-left corner.
    • If you have View PHI permission, additional information, such as the participant's name and date of birth, will be visible.
  3. Select the View Visit Schedule icon in the top-left corner. The Visit Schedule page appears, displaying:
    • Visit
    • Visit Descriptions
    • Status (Due, Overdue, Completed, Missed)
    • Target Date Range
    • Scheduled Start/End Dates and Actual Start/End Dates

  1. Select PRINT to save or print the visit schedule for your records.
  2. To view the audit history of a study visit, select HISTORY on the specific Visit page. The ‘Audit History’ page appears. You can now see all the changes made to the participant’s visit.

Managing non-visit-based forms​

Non-visit-based forms allow you to view supplemental information captured outside the context of scheduled visits.

Steps to view non-visit-based forms​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, select Participant Visit View under ‘Data Entry’. The Participant Visit page appears.
  2. Search and select the participant from the drop-down menu. The participant’s visit details load.
  3. Select NON VISIT-BASED FORMS in the top-right corner.

The list of forms appears.

  1. Select the desired form from the left-side list. The selected record appears in the center of the page.

  2. If required, click the Print button to download the forms in PDF format.

Managing expected and additional CRFs​

You can view expected and additional CRFs for a participant’s visits.

Steps to view expected CRFs​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, select Participant Visit View under ‘Data Entry’. The Participant Visit page appears.
  2. Search and select the participant from the drop-down menu. The participant’s visit details load.
  3. Select the desired visit tab from the top-right. The list of expected CRFs appears on the left, showing their names, statuses, and additional fields.

Managing study CRFs​

The Study CRFs page allows you to view, submit, review, and verify Case Report Forms (CRFs). These forms are categorized into two types:

  • Participant-Specific CRFs: Forms linked to individual participants.
  • Non-Participant-Specific CRFs: Forms not associated with any participant.

The page includes tabs for participant-associated CRFs and non-participant-associated CRFs. When you search for a specific participant, the CRF list and record list automatically filter to show only those related to the participant. ePRO CRFs are marked with a mobile icon for easy identification.

You can access the Study CRFs page from the left menu under the ‘Data Entry’ section.

Viewing records list​

The records list provides details for all participants at the selected site. You can filter the view by selecting a specific participant.

Steps to view the records list​
  1. From the EDC dashboard, select Study CRFs under ‘Data Entry’. The Study CRFs page appears.
  2. Select a CRF from the list on the left. The records associated with the CRF appear.

  1. Scroll to the right of the CRF row and click the Open icon to view the CRF details.

The following table details each record list column, including its description and purpose.

NOTE: Studies can configure additional fields to display in the record list based on their requirements.

Managing form exceptions​

If data cannot be collected on a specific form, such as when a participant refuses an assessment or study staff forget to administer it, the Clinical Site requests a form exception. The Data Coordinating Center reviews these requests periodically and may grant or deny while viewing the CRFs.

Steps to grant a form exception​
  1. Open the CRF you want to grant except.
  2. On the upper-right corner of the CRF, select Grant Exception from the overflow menu.

The Grant Exception dialog appears.

  1. Enter a reason for granting the form exception.
  2. Click OK. The status of the CRF changes to ‘Expectation Removed’.

Steps to deny a form exception​
  1. Open the CRF you want to deny except.
  2. On the upper-right corner of the CRF, select Deny Exception from the overflow menu.

The Deny Exception dialog appears.

  1. Enter a reason for denying the form exception.
  2. Click OK. The status of the CRF changes to ‘TO DO’.

Reviewing CRF fields​

If the fields in the CRF are marked as 'Requiring Review’ in Study Builder, the CRF displays its review status below the CRF name and version number when you view the CRF.

The following table details each CRF Review status, including its description and purpose.

CRF Verification StatusDescription
To ReviewRequires review, but review has not yet begun.
ReviewingRequires review, and one or more fields have been marked as reviewed, but not all.
ReviewedCRF has all fields reviewed.

The following table provides details on field review icons, including its description and purpose.

Field IconDescription
Field review is pending.
Field review is complete
Steps to review fields in CRF​

To review each field marked as requiring review:

  1. Review each question in the CRF and ensure that the entered values are correct to the best of your knowledge.
  2. Click the Review Pending icon located to the right of the entered value for the question.
  • The icon changes to the Review Complete icon after the field is reviewed.
  • The CRF’s verification status changes from ‘TO Review to ‘REVIEWING’.

  1. Review all other questions in CRF. After all the questions in the CRF are reviewed, the CRF review status changes to ‘REVIEWED’.

To review all fields marked as requiring review at once, click the REVIEW ALL button next to the CRF review status.

  • Each question verification icon changes to the Review Complete icon.
  • The CRF’s verification status changes from ‘TO REVIEW to ‘REVIEWED’.

Viewing the CRF Audit History​

The Audit History page records all status updates and actions related to a CRF. The Audit History page includes two sections: ‘CRF Level’ and ‘Question Level’. The ‘CRF Level’ section lists all CRF lockings, unlockings, reviews, and verifications. The ‘Question Level’ section displays the audit history for each field in the CRF.

Steps to view CRF audit history​
  1. Open the CRF you want to view audit history.
  2. On the upper-right corner of the CRF, select History from the overflow menu.

The CRF Audit History dialog appears.

Viewing deleted CRFs​

You can view deleted records associated with a selected CRF by activating the View Deleted CRFs toggle. This includes both deleted and non-deleted records in the list. Deleted CRFs can be viewed or printed as needed.

Steps to view deleted CRFs​

  1. From the EDC dashboard, select Study CRFs under ‘Data Entry’. The Study CRFs page appears.

  2. Select a CRF from the list.

  3. Turn on View Deleted CRFs. The list updates to include deleted records.

NOTE:

  • The View Deleted CRFs toggle is only visible if the CRF has deleted records.
  • Any additional filter criteria entered will also apply to the deleted CRFs displayed.
